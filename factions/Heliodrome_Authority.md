# Heliodrome Authority

The Authority governs a station that is forgetting itself, and it has begun to forget itself in turn. Protocols that once ran automatically now require manual intervention from clerks who no longer remember why the protocols exist. Records that should be permanent have gone soft, their certainty replaced by approximation.

The Authority knows, in the way that institutions know things they cannot afford to say, that it is failing. It continues to issue permits, enforce trade agreements, and adjudicate disputes — not because it believes in its own competence, but because the alternative is a panic that would accelerate the very collapse it is trying to prevent.

In *The Archivist's Wake*, the Authority's dysfunction is visible in the background: the slow response times, the contradictory orders, the officials who ask questions they should already know the answers to.
