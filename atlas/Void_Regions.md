# Void Regions

The Void Regions are not empty in the conventional sense — matter exists, energy flows, physics operates. What is absent is memory-mass: the accumulated weight of things that happened and were remembered. Without it, there is no gravitational reference frame, no corridor to follow, no way for a mind trained to navigate by memory-scent to find its bearings.

Pilots who accidentally enter a Void Region describe the experience as immediate and total: not darkness, not silence, but the sensation of being unmoored from causality itself. The dissociation is not metaphorical. In the absence of memory-gravity, the self begins to lose its coherence.

The Obsidian Synod regards the Void Regions as proof of their theology. The Archivist Orders regard them as a warning.
