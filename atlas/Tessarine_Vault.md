# Tessarine Vault

The Tessarine Vault is a Reliquarist site of the Archivist Orders, attached to the Helios Drift outer sleeve. It is not a single chamber but a vertical complex: an audit hall at the base, a middle ring of newly-sealed reliquary shelves, and an upper bank of long-tone civic-memory silos. The Vault is named for the **Tessarine cradle pattern**, the sixth-generation audit-cradle design codified on-site by the Vault's founders and copied throughout the Reliquarist sect.

## Audit Hall

Sixteen cradle booths in a low-ceilinged hall under the middle ring. The lintel above the outer doors carries the standard doctrine line of the Archivist Orders:

*Spend only what you can afford. Preserve what must endure.*

Cradles are brass, two-armed, fitted with a resonance plate and a forearm-length tuning fork for cross-check. Each Reliquarist tunes their own cradle. The hall is staffed in three watches; juniors and seniors alternate booths. Audit slates and provenance station are placed at the head of the hall for shared use.

## Middle Ring

Shelving for stones that have passed audit and are awaiting transfer to civic silo, family vault, or light-bequest draw. Stones rest here for hours to weeks depending on pledge type.

## Upper Silos

The Helios Drift's long civic memory stones — generations-deep recollections that contribute to the Drift's macrogravitational coherence. These are tuned annually and audited on quarterly review by senior Reliquarists with Navigant Sect oversight.

## Operational Role

The Tessarine Vault handles forty to fifty pledged personal stones per week from the Helios Drift's middle and outer sleeves, most of them light-bequests from elderly donors to surviving family. It also acts as a regional certification site for stones being moved between vaults and as a referral point for stones flagged anomalous on first audit.

## Doctrine and Practice

The Reliquarist sect's procedural reading of the doctrine line is operational: *audit what you can audit, report what you cannot, never seal a stone past your own honest read.* The **junior seal** is a doctrinal exception that allows a junior Reliquarist to certify a stone they cannot fully audit by burning one of their own anchors to log the audit at their personal weight, with the anomaly carried forward for sect review on the next quarterly pass. Junior seals are limited to three per Reliquarist per year and are tracked in the Vault's seal ledger.
