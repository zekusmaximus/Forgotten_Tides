# Kaelen
Continuity Cleric, Archivist Orders  
Station Assignment: Ostra-Vael  

Kaelen is a mid-career Continuity Cleric whose faith in the value of preservation has eroded alongside the station he maintains. His twelve years of daily liturgy have kept Ostra-Vael coherent long past its economic relevance, but the cost has been a slow hollowing of conviction that manifests as accelerated conceptual drift in the station's structure.

## Cognitive Profile

### Strengths
- Deep institutional memory of station architecture and liturgical technique
- Trained focus capable of reinforcing structural coherence without reliquaries
- Disciplined anchor-knot construction (six major knots at story opening)

### Vulnerabilities
- Crisis of faith amplifies the very decay he is trained to prevent
- Willingness to sacrifice personal anchors for institutional preservation
- Post-zero-anchor damage leaves him lighter and more vulnerable to future drift

## Background
Ordained at the High Archives. Assigned to Ostra-Vael during its declining years. Has watched the station's population shrink from three thousand to three hundred over his tenure. His doubt is not philosophical but practical: he questions whether a dying station deserves the cost of being remembered.

## Relationships
- **Vane** (Station Master): Professional respect; she relies on him without fully understanding the cost of his work.
- **Corin** (Maintenance Worker): Represents the ordinary people Kaelen's liturgy protects.
- **Iron Vow crew**: Brief contact with Canticle discipline reminds him of the broader network his work supports.

## Post-Story Status
Two personal anchors permanently burned (mother's face, first love). Zero-anchor damage sustained. Continues to perform the liturgy, but now operates from duty rather than faith—which may, paradoxically, be more honest.
