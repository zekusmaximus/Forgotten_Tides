# Inya Solven
Junior Reliquarist, Archivist Orders  
Station Assignment: Tessarine Vault, Helios Drift outer sleeve  

Inya Solven is twenty-three years old and eleven months into her vows as a junior Reliquarist when the events of *The Reliquarist's Doubt* take place. Brother Hael, her senior, says of her only that she "has hands" — the highest compliment a junior in the audit hall is likely to receive, and the one that matters.

## Cognitive Profile

### Strengths
- Disciplined cradle technique; able to do the deeper read at junior rank without losing tone
- Steady stylus and steady record-keeping under pressure
- Honest self-audit; does not invent reassurance to cover a doubt
- Trained in provenance cross-checking against Vault references

### Vulnerabilities
- Low anchor count for a Reliquarist (four at story start, three after); each junior seal she issues is structurally expensive
- Loyalty to doctrine and to donors pulls in different directions when audits are anomalous
- Inexperience with the political implications of Synod-adjacent flagged sites

## Anchor Structure
Four knots at the opening of story-0008. Three at its close. The burned knot was *"the cold harbor where my mother worked"* — her earliest training knot, made before she had learned to set them properly, and the one she judged most expensive to keep and least expensive to spend.

Junior seal allotment under doctrine: three per year, each carrying its own anchor cost. One used in story-0008.

## Background
Born to a working family in a coastal-adjacent sector of the Helios Drift mid-sleeve. Ordained junior at the Tessarine Vault. Trained under Brother Hael, whose principal teaching she carries forward is that *the cradle does not tell you what a memory is — only whether the memory believes itself.* This becomes the operative line of her audit work and of her conscience.

## Relationships
- **Brother Hael** (senior Reliquarist, mentor): Professional and quietly fond. He noticed something on the morning she came off the Tellis Aru audit and chose not to ask. She noticed that he chose not to ask. Both behaviors are doctrine, and both are also kindness.
- **Tellis Aru** (donor, never met): The 71-year-old Sleeve Four citizen whose pledge stone Inya audited. Inya thinks of her at the close of the story as someone whose pledge she has matched, knot for knot, in the dark.

## Narrative Function
Inya embodies the *duty-vs-faith* axis of the Archivist Orders' interior life. She is not a heretic, a whistleblower, or a martyr. She is a junior who has read the doctrine carefully enough to find the seam in it, and who chooses to spend her own weight rather than pass the cost back onto a dying donor. The Orders' system anticipated that exactly this would happen — the junior seal exists for it — but the system did not name the cost; she names it, and pays.

## Post-Story Status
Three anchors remaining. One junior seal of three spent. The anomalous pledge stone is logged in the quarterly review queue for Navigant Sect attention twelve weeks hence; she does not know what they will do with it, and the story closes before she finds out. Her arc is open for continuation.
